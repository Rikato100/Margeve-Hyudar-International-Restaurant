Best Selling Filipino Foods
Sample Image Name Description
Pork Sinigang Pork sinigang is one of the most popular dishes in Filipino cuisine and its slightly sour flavor makes it stand out and very memorable. It is a sour soup with pork ribs, vegetables, and tamarind-flavored broth. It's hearty, tasty, and perfect for cold weather.
Pancit Palabok Pancit Palabok is a noodle dish with shrimp sauce and topped with several ingredients such as cooked shrimp, boiled pork, crushed chicharon, tinapa flakes, fried tofu, scallions, and fried garlic.
Kare-Kare Kare-kare is a traditional Filipino stew with a thick, savory peanut sauce. It's made with a base of stewed meat, vegetables, and a peanut sauce, and is often served with white rice. It is often made with shrimp paste on the side. Some say the dish is a little sweet because of the peanut butter, and is often eaten with salty shrimp paste.
Leche Flan Leche Flan is a dessert made-up of eggs and milk with a soft caramel on top. It resembles crème caramel and caramel custard.
Halo-Halo or Mix-mix Halo-halo is one the most beloved and favorite summertime desserts. From the Philippines—the name means “mix-mix" in Tagalog—the refreshing dish layers shaved ice and condensed milk on top of all sorts of ingredients for an end result that packs in lots of contrasting textures, from chewy to crunchy, creamy to sticky.
