Best Selling Japanese Foods
Sample Image Name Description
Yaki Udon Yaki udon is a Japanese dish of stir-fried udon noodles, vegetables, and meat or seafood in a sweet and savory sauce. The name translates to "fried udon," with "yaki" meaning "fry" or "grill". Yaki udon is similar to yakisoba, another popular Japanese stir-fried noodle dish. Both are often served at Japanese festivals.
Karaage Karaage is a Japanese cooking technique that involves marinating a protein or vegetable, dredging it in flour, and then deep-frying it.
Sushi Sushi is a Japanese dish made from seasoned rice, fish, egg, or vegetables, and shaped into a roll with a thin sheet of seaweed. The word "sushi" comes from a Japanese word that means "sour rice".
Takoyaki A popular street food made from bite-sized pieces of octopus in batter and served with toppings.
Sashimi Sashimi is a Japanese dish of thinly sliced raw seafood or other meats. It is often served with daikon radish, pickled ginger, wasabi, or soy sauce. Sashimi is commonly made with fish like salmon, tuna, halibut, and squid. However, other meats, such as beef, horse, and deer, can also be served as sashimi.
